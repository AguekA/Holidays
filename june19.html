Juneteenth

Two facts about Juneteenth:

  • Fact 1: Juneteenth marks the day when Union General Gordon Granger arrived in Galveston, Texas, on June 19, 1865, and announced the end of slavery. This occurred more than two years after the Emancipation Proclamation was signed by President Abraham Lincoln.
  • Fact 2: Juneteenth represents a significant milestone in the struggle for equality and the fight against slavery in the United States. It symbolizes the end of one of the darkest chapters in American history and the triumph of freedom.
